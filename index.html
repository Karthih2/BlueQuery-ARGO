OHC Methodology
Formula: OHC = ρ × Cp × ∫T(z) dz
Ocean Heat Content (OHC) quantifies the thermal energy stored in the water column. We use the standard formula: OHC = ρ × Cp × ∫T(z) dz, where ρ = 1025 kg/m³ (seawater density), Cp = 3985 J/kg/°C (specific heat). The integral is computed numerically using the trapezoidal rule over depth levels from the Argo float profile. Results are expressed in MJ/m². The thermocline depth shown marks the maximum temperature gradient (dT/dz), separating the surface mixed layer from the thermocline zone.
